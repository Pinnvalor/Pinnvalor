Akme Fintrade and Kesoram Industries: Navigating Troubling Declines in India’s Stock Market

  • Prasoon Jain
  • April 27, 2025

Akme Fintrade (India) Ltd: A Volatile Journey

Akme Fintrade (India) Ltd., a non-banking financial company (NBFC) focusing on rural and semi-urban lending, has experienced sharp volatility and declining stock performance over recent months. The company, which initially had high hopes for its market debut, has seen its stock price nosedive from a listing price of ₹127 in June 2024 to ₹8.34 as of April 2025.

Stock Performance Overview

Akme Fintrade (India) Ltd., a non-banking financial company (NBFC) focusing on rural and semi-urban lending, has experienced sharp volatility and declining stock performance over recent months. The company, which initially had high hopes for its market debut, has seen its stock price nosedive from a listing price of ₹127 in June 2024 to ₹8.34 as of April 2025.

The struggles of Akme Fintrade and Kesoram Industries reveal the risks of poor financial management and external challenges. Akme’s high NPAs and regulatory issues, combined with Kesoram’s mounting losses and an uncertain acquisition, underline the importance of robust governance and careful risk assessment for investors navigating a volatile market.

Factors Influencing the Decline

  • High Non-Performing Assets (NPAs): Non-performing assets have been a major issue for Akme Fintrade, with a significant portion of its loan portfolio becoming overdue. This has negatively impacted the company’s ability to generate stable cash flows, leading to financial instability.
  • Regulatory Concerns: Akme Fintrade has come under the scrutiny of the Reserve Bank of India (RBI) for non-compliance with regulatory norms. The company has faced issues with its management structure, which has seen abrupt changes without the required approvals.
  • Market Sentiment: Despite the company’s initial listing and the excitement surrounding its IPO, Akme Fintrade’s stock has failed to maintain momentum, leading to investor uncertainty and a significant sell-off.

Kesoram Industries Ltd: Facing Structural and Market Pressures

Stock Performance Overview:

Kesoram Industries Ltd., a diversified conglomerate with interests in cement and textiles, is another company that has been experiencing significant downward pressure on its stock. Once seen as a stable performer, Kesoram’s stock has witnessed a dramatic decline, reaching a 52-week low of ₹4.50 by March 2025.

Key Challenges

  • Block Deals and Stake Sales: A significant factor contributing to Kesoram’s troubles has been the selling of large blocks of stock by institutional investors. Recently, a 3.5% equity block deal was executed, which led to increased selling pressure on the stock.
  • Financial Performance: Kesoram has struggled with widening losses, which have deepened investor concerns. The company reported a net loss of ₹69.17 crore in Q3FY25, a staggering figure that shocked many analysts.
  • Acquisition by UltraTech Cement: Kesoram has been in the process of negotiating an acquisition by UltraTech Cement. This acquisition has created uncertainty for investors, as the terms of the deal have yet to be finalized.

Conclusion: What Lies Ahead for Akme Fintrade and Kesoram Industries?

Both Akme Fintrade and Kesoram Industries are navigating challenging and uncertain times in the Indian stock market. For Akme Fintrade, the road to recovery lies in addressing its NPA issues and ensuring full compliance with regulatory norms. Investors will be watching closely to see if the company can stabilize its finances and regain market confidence.

For Kesoram Industries, the focus will likely be on how the UltraTech Cement acquisition unfolds and whether the company can restructure its operations effectively. With high debt levels and a shrinking market share, Kesoram needs a strategic turnaround to avoid further losses.

In the meantime, investors should proceed with caution when considering these stocks, as both companies face considerable risks. Continued monitoring of regulatory developments, management decisions, and market conditions will be crucial in determining the future prospects of these once-promising stocks.

Are high NPAs and regulatory challenges the tipping point for Akme Fintrade, or can it turn its fortunes around?
