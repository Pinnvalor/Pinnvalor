IAS 32 is an International Accounting Standard issued by the International Accounting Standards Board (IASB). It focuses exclusively on the presentation of financial instruments‚Äînamely, how to classify financial assets, liabilities, and equity instruments on the face of the financial statements.

IAS 32 applies to all entities and all types of financial instruments except for:

1. üîç Liability vs Equity Classification

A central element of IAS 32 is determining whether a financial instrument is a financial liability or an equity instrument.

Instruments such as convertible bonds contain both debt and equity components. IAS 32 mandates splitting them at initial recognition:

  • The residual is allocated to equity.

This treatment ensures faithful representation of both the debt obligation and the conversion right.

IAS 32 permits offsetting only when:

Such instruments can be classified as equity, despite the redemption feature, if certain strict conditions are met.
